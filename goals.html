Where I'm Going

the long view

I want to work at the intersection of security and systems engineering — building software that is not just functional, but defensible. Whether that means working on backend infrastructure at a security company, doing applied research in binary analysis, or building tools that help defenders understand how attackers think, I want my work to matter beyond just shipping features.

I'm currently a sophomore, which means I have enough experience to be useful and enough runway to be deliberate. The plan below is my current thinking — it'll evolve, but it's grounded in the skills and experiences I know I need.

Land a Technical Internship

My immediate goal is securing a summer 2026 SWE or security engineering internship. I'm targeting roles where the work is technical and the codebase matters — backend systems, security tooling, or infrastructure.

  • Sharpening Python, distributed systems fundamentals, and system design for technical interviews
  • Deepening reverse engineering skills through continued malware analysis work
  • Building a polished GitHub presence with documented projects
Junior Year — 2026/2027
Go Deeper on Security + Systems

With an internship behind me, I want coursework and research that pushes my technical depth in the areas I care about most.

  • Pursue an undergraduate research position in a security or systems lab at GT
  • Return internship at a security-focused company or move to a larger role
Senior Year — 2027
Capstone + Career Decision

Senior year is about synthesis — pulling together four years of coursework, projects, and internship experience into a clear professional identity.

  • Complete a substantial senior capstone in a security or systems domain
  • Decide between direct industry entry and a master's/PhD in security
  • Target roles at security companies, product companies with strong security orgs, or research labs
